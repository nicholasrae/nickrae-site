## Verdict

The site builds credibility excellently and converts it into nothing.
Your strongest content (Kalshi bot post) has no path to your strongest
product (Playbook). Your strongest product is item #8 on the homepage,
below word search books. Your strongest trust signal (the $340 mistake)
lives in a blog post instead of on the sales page. The conversion
architecture is: arrive, be impressed, have nowhere to go.

---

## Biggest Conversion Leak

The Kalshi weather bot post. It's likely your highest-traffic page
(specific topic, real numbers, the kind of thing that gets shared in
AI/quant communities). Someone finishes reading it and sees three CTAs:
Flight Funded tracker, PPL Workbook, IR Workbook. None of these are
relevant. The natural next action is "how do I build one of these?"
The answer is the Playbook. It's not linked. Every day this post runs
without a Playbook CTA is conversion left on the table.

## Best Existing Strength

Honesty as a brand. The $340 mistake. The BOUNDARY_FADE -$251 loss.
The 57.9% live WR next to the 76.5% paper WR. The "What I Got Wrong"
sections. Most personal sites cherry-pick wins. Yours documents
failures with dollar amounts. This is genuinely rare and it's doing
more trust-building than any testimonial could. Don't sanitize it.

## 1. Clarity

### Immediately understandable:
- You're a pilot building things to fund flight hours. Clear in 10 seconds.
- You're not a developer. This comes through naturally and is a selling point.
- You build real things with real results. Numbers everywhere.

### Confusing, buried, or competing:
- **9 projects at equal visual weight.** FeedFare (your primary app focus)
  and the Playbook (your $99 product) are lost in a list that includes
  word search books and a nonprofit. A visitor can't tell which things
  matter to you and which are side projects.
- **The Playbook is only reachable through the blog.** No nav item. No
  homepage callout. Project #8 of 9.
- **FeedFare's positioning is broken** (per the FeedFare memo: promises
  app locking, delivers an honor system). Sending homepage traffic to
  an app with a positioning contradiction hurts overall site credibility.
- **The blog index is a flat chronological list.** The Kalshi post and
  Playbook post are your two highest-value pages and they sit alongside
  everything else with no visual distinction.

### Focused or scattered:
Scattered. The site reads as "here is everything I've ever done" rather
than "here is what I can do for you." The resume/portfolio framing serves
your airline job search. The product framing serves revenue. They're
competing on the same page.

## 3. CTA Strength

### Pages with strong next steps:
- **Playbook blog post:** Stats bar, interior previews (on blog, not Gumroad),
  discount code, Gumroad link. Needs a second CTA earlier in the page.
  Otherwise the strongest conversion page on the site.
- **Gumroad listing:** Clear price, 30-day guarantee, instant delivery.
  Needs interior previews and social proof.

### Pages that leak intent:
- **Kalshi bot post:** Reader finishes impressed, gets pointed to PPL
  Workbook. The Playbook CTA is absent. This is the single biggest leak.
- **Homepage:** 4 hero buttons, none of which lead to a purchase. The
  visitor must scroll through 8 projects to find the Playbook.
- **Cron jobs guide post:** 12 minutes of deep BOFU content. Has a
  Playbook CTA at the end. But it gives away so much for free that
  the marginal value of the Playbook feels lower. This post should
  tease the depth, not deliver it.
- **Blog index:** Flat list, no featured posts, no indication of what's
  most popular or most important. Reader has to guess.

### Where readers likely stall:
- Homepage after the "What I'm Building" section. They've scanned 9
  projects and none had a clear "buy this" moment. The aviation
  credentials and work history below are only relevant to hiring
  managers.
- Kalshi bot post after "What's Next." The post ends with a reflection
  on the journey, not a conversion opportunity.
- Blog index. 13 undifferentiated posts. No guidance on where to start.

3. Add the $340 story higher on the Playbook sales page

## Top 5 Conversion Fixes

### 1. Add Playbook CTA to the Kalshi bot post.

Add a callout box after "What's Next": "The full bot setup, configs,
cron schedules, and the mistakes that cost me $340 are in The
Non-Developer's OpenClaw Playbook. [Get it on Gumroad — $99]."

Also add a brief mention at the top: "This bot was built with
OpenClaw. The full system is in the Playbook."

**Time:** 15 minutes. **Impact:** Connects your best traffic source
to your best product.

### 2. Fix all inconsistent numbers across every page.

The playbook is 123 pages. Use 123 everywhere. The live win rate is
57.9%. Use 57.9% everywhere. Go through Gumroad listing, blog post,
homepage description, and marketing site. Make every number match
reality.

**Time:** 30 minutes. **Impact:** Stops the fastest trust-killer
for any buyer doing basic due diligence.

### 3. Add Playbook to top nav and promote to project #1 or #2 on homepage.

Add "Playbook" as a nav item linking to the blog post or directly
to Gumroad. On the homepage, move it above FeedFare and give it
the stats bar (47 crons, $30/mo, $352 revenue, 0 code) inline.
Remove puzzle books from the homepage.

**Time:** 45 minutes. **Impact:** Your highest-margin product
becomes findable.

### 4. Upload interior previews and free sample link to Gumroad.

You already have 3 images (cover, TOC, security chapter) and a
16-page free sample PDF. Upload all of them to Gumroad. Add 1-2
more: the cron config page and the Kalshi bot chapter opener.

**Time:** 20 minutes. **Impact:** Answers "what am I getting for
$99?" which is the #1 purchase objection.

### 5. Cross-link every technical blog post to the Playbook.

The setup guide, 30-day review, and cron jobs guide all build
toward the Playbook but only the cron jobs guide links to it.
Add a consistent footer to all OpenClaw blog posts: "These posts
cover the highlights. The full system, configs, and mistakes are
in the Playbook." Link to Gumroad.

**Time:** 20 minutes. **Impact:** Every blog reader hits at least
one Playbook touchpoint.

## Summary

Total time for all 5 fixes: ~2 hours.

The site's problem is not traffic, design, or content quality.
The content is strong. The problem is plumbing. Your best product
is hidden. Your best content doesn't link to it. Your best proof
points are scattered across pages instead of concentrated where
the purchase decision happens. Two hours of restructuring would
do more for revenue than two months of new content.
