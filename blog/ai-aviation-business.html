How I'm Using AI to Build Side Income as a Pilot

I need 108.7 more flight hours to reach the 500-hour Part 135 target. At roughly $200/hr, that's about $21,740. I'm using AI automation and shipped aviation products to close the gap while keeping app experiments parked and market research paper-only.

I'm a commercial pilot (ASEL, AMEL, Instrument) with 391.3 total hours. My day job is managing a mobile electronics shop in Merced, CA. I'm not flying full time yet. The goal is to get to 500 hours for the next Part 135 threshold, and eventually 1,500 for ATP minimums. That takes money I don't have sitting around.

So I'm building income streams on the side, and using AI automation to do most of the heavy lifting while I'm at work. Here's an honest accounting of where things stand.

The Stack: Hermes Agent + Talos

I originally ran this operating system through OpenClaw. That runtime is now retired. The current stack uses Hermes Agent, with my agent Talos handling bounded research, local builds, verification, and scheduled monitoring on my Mac.

  • Content creation: Blog posts, SEO optimization, marketing copy
  • Code generation: Helps build and maintain my iOS apps
  • Market research: Scans for opportunities, analyzes competitors
  • Evidence management: Separates shipped proof, drafts, paper results, and real revenue
  • Scheduled monitoring: Runs a bounded set of health, evidence, and briefing jobs

Talos isn't magic. It's infrastructure. The value comes from having a system that moves projects forward during the hours I can't.

What I'm Building (Honest Status)

KDP Books — Live, First Revenue

The aviation catalog now has five Amazon workbooks, with PPL and Instrument carrying the clearest current sales signal:

  • Private Pilot License Workbook: 500+ practice questions, $19.99 print / $9.99 Kindle
  • Instrument Rating Workbook: Comprehensive IFR prep, $24.99 print / $9.99 Kindle
  • The Complete Commercial Pilot Workbook: written test and oral exam prep for commercial candidates
  • Multi-Engine Add-On Rating Workbook: PA-44-focused V-speeds, Vmc, OEI, and oral prep
  • Clearance to Confidence: VFR Radio Calls Workbook: scenario-based call-building and readback practice

All five are now collected on the Aviation Pilot Workbook Series page so the book funnel has one clean destination instead of scattered product links.

Reconciled tracking through July 22 puts the aviation workbook lane at 110 units, $952.38 USD plus €37.42 EUR. That is tracked aviation-product proof across the stated March-July evidence windows, not an Amazon Ads attribution claim. Instrument carries the clearest current ad signal, while PPL still shows durable product demand. Still small, but no longer imaginary.

FeedFare — Live on the App Store

An iOS app where you trade steps for screen time. Set a daily walking goal, earn minutes, and use movement to build better phone habits.

FeedFare is live on the App Store and remains a separate product experiment. I do not have verified revenue proof for it. Further conversion and App Store work is parked unless the lane is explicitly reopened.

Kalshi Weather Bot — Paper Trading

The current credential-free research stack collects public Kalshi weather-market, NBM, and METAR evidence. It is a paper/shadow research system, not a live trading product.

An earlier paper headline failed harder market-relative validation. The settled V1 ledger finished 1 win, 19 losses, and -$24.99 in realized paper P&L. V2 is collecting under stricter abstention rules and has not produced a candidate yet. Live trading is off. The useful lesson is that automation still needs brutal gates.

Other Apps — Parked

Inflection Point and older app experiments such as SpendSlap and PetFace are parked. They are not active revenue lanes, and unfinished software is not a product merely because AI generated code for it.

YouTube — Not Yet Monetized

My YouTube channel documents the journey. AI helps with content planning, script drafts, and thumbnail concepts. It's not generating income yet. I'm treating it as a long-term play and a transparency tool for the Flight Funded experiment.

What AI Actually Does Well

After several months of running this system, here's what genuinely works:

  • Bounded background work: Talos monitors evidence, checks system health, and prepares concise decision briefs without pretending activity is progress.
  • Research at scale: Competitive analysis, niche scouting for KDP, SEO keyword research. Tasks that would take me hours take minutes.
  • Code generation: Going from idea to working iOS app in days instead of months. I'm not a developer by training. AI closed that gap.
  • Content production: Blog posts, product descriptions, social media drafts. The volume would be impossible solo.

What AI Doesn't Do

  • Ship products by itself: Public releases, account changes, purchases, and outreach still require human judgment and approval.
  • Build an audience: No shortcut for genuine engagement. Reviews, followers, and trust take time.
  • Replace judgment: The weather research stack defaults to abstention because blindly trusting model signals with real money is how you go broke.
  • Guarantee income: KDP royalties are real. Paper P&L, app code, clicks, and unfinished products are not revenue. Still early, no fairy dust.

The Math

The near-term target is 500 hours for the Part 135 path. At 391.3 hours, that leaves 108.7 hours. At roughly $200/hr, that is about a $21,740 gap. ATP minimums are the later mountain, but the next hill is 500 hours.

The cleanest signal right now is aviation products: reconciled tracking through July 22 is 110 aviation units and $952.38 USD plus €37.42 EUR. The workbook catalog is the lead revenue lane. FeedFare is live but unproven and parked. Weather-market work stays paper-only until independent evidence beats the market after costs.

What's Next

  1. Sell what is already shipped: The aviation workbooks need distribution and customer proof more than another product draft.
  2. Hold ad scale: Keep the converting Instrument campaign capped while same-window royalty economics mature.
  3. Pursue paid flying honestly: Apply only where the current posting and verified logbook fit.
  4. Keep weather research gated: No live restart until independent forward evidence beats the market after costs.
  5. Leave parked products parked: No app resurrection without a buyer, user, or explicit proof gate.

I'm tracking every dollar publicly on my Flight Funded dashboard. Real numbers, real time, no spin. If AI-powered side income can fund a pilot career, you'll see it happen there first.

The honest version of this story isn't "AI made me rich." It's "AI gave me leverage I didn't have before, and now I'm trying to turn that leverage into something real." I'll let you know how it goes.
